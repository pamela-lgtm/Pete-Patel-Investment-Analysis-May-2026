📋 Your 1031 Exchange Framework

With your Philadelphia property listing in early June 2026, the 1031 timeline below applies. To fully defer capital gains, the replacement property must be of equal or greater value than your Philadelphia sale, all proceeds must be reinvested, and any cash held back becomes "boot" subject to tax. Mortgage debt on the replacement substitutes for any debt that was paid off on the Philly property.

Recommended Next Steps

A 1031-specific path from this analysis to identification and acquisition.

1Confirm Your Philadelphia Numbers with Your CPA

Before formal identification, work with your CPA to confirm:

  • Expected Philadelphia sale price (your minimum replacement target)
  • Outstanding Philly mortgage balance (your minimum new debt target for full deferral)
  • Total transaction costs and expected net proceeds (your cash position)
  • Whether you have a qualified intermediary engaged — required to hold proceeds during exchange

2Verandah Rental Comp Confirmation — Top 3

Before formal identification, Verandah pulls independent rental comps within 0.5–1 mile of your top 3. Converts directional yield numbers into underwritable income figures so your cash flow assumptions are defensible:

  • Sunridge: Suncrest 2BR townhouse rental velocity at $2,100–2,150
  • Sunkissed: already partially validated via sister-unit live ask at $2,150 — supporting comps from Verandah
  • Citruswood: Lake Nona 4BR townhouse rental at $2,650–2,800 with 1st-floor primary premium

3Lender Pre-Approval (If Not Already in Place)

You'll need investor mortgage pre-approval for any property above $300K — which is 9 of the 11. Recommend pre-approval well before identification deadline. Verandah can introduce you to local investor-focused lenders who understand 1031 timelines.

4Three-Property Identification Strategy

The IRS "three property rule" is the safe-harbor approach for 1031 identification. Recommend identifying:

  • Primary target — Sunridge Loop ($315,000): best AVM discount, smallest mortgage, REO seller motivated to clear
  • Backup target — Sunkissed Drive ($304,990): cleanest transaction, fastest close, near-all-cash deployment
  • Stretch target — Citruswood Drive ($359,990): highest yield, true Lake Nona address, larger upside if mortgage rate cooperates

If primary falls through, you have two qualified backups within identification — protecting your tax deferral.

5Offer Strategy & Sequencing

  • First offer — Sunridge at $290–295K cash, 45-day close: Fannie Mae HomePath via RES.NET portal, full proof of funds. Realistic counter range $300–308K. Mortgage drops to ~$0.
  • If Sunridge falls through — Sunkissed at $290–298K cash, 30-day close: Private seller, smoothest transaction. Counter range $295–302K.
  • If both fall through — Citruswood at $342–348K cash, 30-day close: LLC investor seller. Counter range $350–355K. Larger mortgage component but still under $50K.

6Verandah's Role Through Closing & Beyond

  • Rental comp confirmation and underwriting support
  • On-site walkthroughs of identified properties
  • Coordination with your qualified intermediary and lender on 1031 timeline
  • Offer preparation and contingency drafting (1031 contingency clause is essential)
  • Post-close: lease-up marketing, tenant screening, full property management
  • SureVestor Landlord Shield enrollment for GL coverage with no deductible
  • Experian rent reporting via AppFolio for tenant retention
